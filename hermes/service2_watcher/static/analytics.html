XGBoost Model — Next-Day Return Forecast

Hermes trains a per-symbol XGBoost regressor on 10 alpha features derived from daily and intraday price/volume bars. The model predicts the next-day return (%) for each symbol in the watchlist. Retrains weekly; predicts daily.

Feature Dictionary — the 10 Alpha Inputs
① Overnight Gap
(open − prev_close) / prev_close
Measures the gap at open vs. prior close. Large gaps often mean-revert intraday.
② Vol-Norm 5d Momentum
(close − close₋₅) / (σ₂₀ · close₋₅)
5-day price momentum scaled by 20-day realized vol. Removes vol-regime bias.
③ SPY Beta Residual
ret − β₆₀ · SPY_ret
Symbol return minus SPY beta contribution. Isolates idiosyncratic alpha from market drift.
④ Intraday Return
(close − open) / open
Full day return from open to close. Captures directional conviction within the session.
⑤ VWAP Distance
(close − VWAP₃:₅₉) / close
How far the close deviated from closing-period VWAP. Positive = closed above institutional average.
⑥ Range Position
(close − low) / (high − low)
Where the close sits within the day's range. 1.0 = closed at high; 0.0 = closed at low.
⑦ Volume Z-Score (20d)
(vol − SMA₂₀) / σ₂₀
How unusual today's volume is vs. the 20-day average. High z-score = elevated conviction.
⑧ Last-30min Volume %
vol₁₅:₃₀–₁₆:₀₀ / vol_total
Fraction of daily volume traded in the closing 30 minutes. Smart money tends to act late.
⑨ Realized Vol 5d (ann.)
σ(log_ret₅) × √252
Short-term annualised volatility. Rising vol often precedes mean-reversion opportunities.
⑩ Seasonality
day_of_week, month
Day-of-week (0=Mon) and calendar month. Captures Monday effect, January effect, OpEx patterns.

K-Means S/R Clustering — Structural Market Analysis

Key Levels are identified by running K-Means Clustering on local price pivots. Pivots are weighted by Volume × Recency² to prioritize high-conviction, recent price action. The algorithm automatically clusters these pivots into 6 significant zones.

Calculation Logic
Pivot Identification
Window = 5 bars
Uses argrelextrema to find local minima/maxima within a 5-day rolling window.
Recency Bias
Weight ∝ (index / total)²
Square of the relative index ensures recent pivots have exponentially more influence than old ones.
Clustering
K = 6
Groups thousands of historical data points into 6 distinct price levels (Support/Resistance).
Level Strength
Cluster Density
Strength is determined by the number of individual pivots that fall into the same cluster.
